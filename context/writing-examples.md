## Example 1: Article Opening (Lead with Data, No Fluff)

**Source**: "The Complete Guide to Selling on Amazon Mexico in 2026"
**URL**: https://tallyglobal.ai/blog/sell-on-amazon-mexico-complete-guide.html
**Primary Keyword**: sell on amazon mexico

**What Makes It Great**:
- Primary keyword appears in the first sentence
- Leads with three specific data points ($54.4 billion, $175.8 billion, 34% YoY) before making any claim
- Addresses the "catch" (SAT requirement) immediately -- no hiding the complexity
- Ends with a credibility anchor (4,500+ companies, 20+ countries)
- Zero fluff. No "In this article we will discuss..." opening.

**Excerpt**:

```
If you want to sell on Amazon Mexico, 2026 is the year to move. Mexico's
ecommerce market reached $54.4 billion in 2025 (IMARC Group) and is projected
to hit $175.8 billion by 2034. Amazon Mexico specifically is growing at 34%
year-over-year, outpacing every other Amazon marketplace on the planet. And
with 130 million consumers, 79% internet penetration, and a fraction of the
seller competition you face in the US or EU, the window is wide open.

But there is a catch. Since January 2025, Mexico's tax authority (SAT) requires
all Amazon sellers to have a legal representative in Mexico. That means you need
a Mexican entity, a tax ID, a local bank account, and the right compliance
framework before you list a single product. No shortcuts.

This guide covers everything: the legal requirements, a week-by-week timeline,
real costs, COFEPRIS regulations, and the mistakes that derail first-time
sellers. Tally Global has formed over 4,500 companies in Mexico for brands from
20+ countries. This is what we know works.
```

**Voice Notes**:
- "No shortcuts." -- Two words, full stop. This is the Tally voice: direct, no BS.
- "This is what we know works." -- Confident without being arrogant. Experience-based authority.
- Every paragraph has specific numbers. No vague claims.

## Example 2: Regulatory Explanation (Complex Topic Made Clear)

**Source**: "The Complete Guide to Selling on Amazon Mexico in 2026"
**Section**: COFEPRIS overview and product category table

**What Makes It Great**:
- Explains COFEPRIS with a one-line analogy ("Mexico's equivalent of the US FDA")
- Uses a structured table for timeline/process comparisons instead of dense paragraphs
- Addresses the reader directly ("If your products touch the human body")
- Internal link to service page with descriptive anchor text

**Excerpt**:

```
COFEPRIS (Comision Federal para la Proteccion contra Riesgos Sanitarios) is
Mexico's health regulatory authority -- the equivalent of the US FDA. If your
products touch the human body (cosmetics, supplements, food) or the medical
field (devices, pharmaceuticals), you need COFEPRIS clearance before importing.

[TABLE: Product Category | COFEPRIS Required? | Process | Timeline]
Cosmetics        | Yes | Sanitary notification (Aviso de Funcionamiento) | 3-15 days
Supplements      | Yes | Registro Sanitario                              | 3-6 months
Food & beverages | Yes | Aviso de Funcionamiento + NOM-051 labeling      | 2-4 weeks
Medical devices  | Yes | Registro Sanitario (Class I, II, or III)        | 6-18 months
Electronics      | No (COFEPRIS) | NOM certification required             | Varies
```

**Voice Notes**:
- The Mexican regulatory term is given first, then the English explanation
- "If your products touch the human body" -- plain language that instantly clarifies scope
- Table provides scannable, specific timelines instead of vague generalizations
- No attempt to make COFEPRIS sound easy or hard -- just factual

## Style Patterns to Replicate

### Pattern 1: Data Sandwich
Open with a data point, make the claim, close with supporting data.
> "Mexico's ecommerce market reached $54.4 billion in 2025 and is projected to hit $175.8 billion by 2034. That is a 222% increase over the next decade."

### Pattern 2: Analogy Bridge
Introduce the Mexican term, then bridge with a US/international equivalent.
> "You need an RFC (Registro Federal de Contribuyentes), which is Mexico's equivalent of an EIN."
> "COFEPRIS is Mexico's health regulatory authority -- the equivalent of the US FDA."

### Pattern 3: Catch and Address
Present the opportunity, then immediately acknowledge the challenge.
> "Amazon Mexico is growing at 34% year-over-year... But there is a catch. Since January 2025, SAT requires all Amazon sellers to have a legal representative in Mexico."

### Pattern 4: Specific Over Generic
Always choose the specific version.
> Good: "Entity formation takes 4-6 weeks. Banking runs in parallel at 2-4 weeks."
> Bad: "The process takes a few weeks to a few months depending on various factors."

### Pattern 5: Short Punch for Emphasis
After a longer explanatory passage, use a short declarative sentence for impact.
> "No shortcuts."
> "This is what we know works."
> "Start early."
> "It is not a sunk cost."

### Pattern 6: Table for Comparison
Whenever comparing 3+ items on 3+ dimensions, use a table instead of prose.
> See the Amazon Mexico vs Mercado Libre comparison, the cost breakdown, and the COFEPRIS timeline tables in the guide.

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

### Anti-Pattern 1: The Fluff Intro
> BAD: "In today's rapidly evolving digital landscape, businesses are increasingly looking to expand their horizons into new and exciting markets. One such market that has been garnering significant attention is Mexico..."

### Anti-Pattern 2: Vague Claims
> BAD: "Our team of experts can help you navigate the complex regulatory landscape in a timely and cost-effective manner."

### Anti-Pattern 3: Feature Dump Without Benefit
> BAD: "Tally offers entity formation, COFEPRIS registration, banking, accounting, imports, and marketplace access."
> GOOD: "Tally handles everything from entity formation to first sale. One team, one point of contact, 8-12 weeks."

### Anti-Pattern 4: Generic CTA
> BAD: "Contact us to learn more about our services."
> GOOD: "Get your Mexico roadmap. Plans start at $588/month, entity formation included."
