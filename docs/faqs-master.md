## CATEGORY 1: ENTITY FORMATION

### Q: How long does it take to form a Mexican company?
**A:** The full process takes 4-6 weeks from document collection to receiving your RFC and eFirma. This includes company name reservation (1 week), notarization (1 week), Registro Publico inscription (1-2 weeks), and SAT registration (1 week). All steps are managed by Tally — you sign remotely via DocuSign.

### Q: Which entity type should I choose — S de RL, SA, or SAPI?
**A:** Most Amazon and Mercado Libre sellers choose an **S de RL de CV** for its flexibility with foreign shareholders, no revenue cap, and optional statutory examiner. For investor-backed ventures with complex governance needs, a **SAPI de CV** offers more sophisticated share transfer mechanisms. SA de CV is recommended for traditional businesses. Tally advises on the optimal structure based on your ownership, revenue projections, and operational needs.

### Q: Do I need to be physically present in Mexico to form a company?
**A:** No. The entire process can be completed remotely. Shareholders sign all documents via DocuSign, and identity is verified through MetaMap's digital verification system. Tally handles all in-person requirements at the notary, SAT office, and Registro Publico on your behalf.

### Q: Do I own the company 100%?
**A:** Yes. Unlike merchant-of-record services or marketplace aggregators, you are the registered shareholder and legal owner. The entity, its RFC, bank accounts, marketplace seller accounts, and all permits belong to you. If you ever change service providers, everything stays with you. This is the core Tally difference.

### Q: Does the initial capital need to be deposited into a Mexican bank?
**A:** No. The initial capital contribution does not need to be deposited into a Mexican bank account. It is sufficient that the contribution is properly documented in the company's incorporation documents. This can be done through a cash contribution or other form, as long as it is legally recorded. There are no mandatory ongoing capital contributions beyond the initial amount.
*Source: Real client question answered by Ximena/Uriel*

### Q: How was the company domicile selected? Can I choose where it's registered?
**A:** The legal domicile (domicilio social) is set by default in a location with lower government registration fees to offer a more cost-effective setup. Changing the legal domicile to Mexico City or another location requires a formal corporate amendment (~$2,850 MXN additional). However, the **fiscal domicile** (domicilio fiscal) — the address registered with SAT, used for invoicing and compliance — is set in Mexico City (CDMX) by default. This is the address vendors, partners, and tax authorities will see. In practice, the legal domicile has limited relevance compared to the fiscal domicile.
*Source: Real client question answered by Ximena/Uriel*

### Q: Can profits be distributed to the U.S. without restrictions?
**A:** Yes, but subject to Mexican tax obligations:
- **30% Corporate Income Tax (ISR)** on company profits, paid before dividends
- **10% Withholding Tax** on dividends to foreign shareholders (may be reduced to 5% under the US-Mexico tax treaty if the shareholder owns 10%+ and provides a valid tax residency certificate)
- **Example:** Pre-tax profit $1,000 → ISR $300 → After-tax $700 → Withholding $70 → Net dividend $630
*Source: Real client question answered by Ximena/Uriel*

### Q: Can we dissolve the company at any time?
**A:** Yes. Two approaches:
- **Simplified Dissolution** (after 8 months of paid representation): One-time $900 USD covering 2 years of suspension, required filings, and full dissolution process. Monthly option available at $50 USD/mo for 24 months. Company can be reactivated during suspension.
- **Traditional Dissolution:** Formal notarial process with higher costs. Not offered by Tally.
Requirements: Publish shareholder structure in PSM 15+ business days in advance, be fully compliant with tax/labor/social security obligations, file final tax return.
*Source: Real client question answered by Ximena/Uriel*

### Q: Why is a domestic RFC better than a foreign RFC for selling in Mexico?
**A:** A domestic RFC (through a Mexican entity) is superior in almost every way:
- **Simpler compliance:** No permanent establishment risk, no double-taxation complexity
- **Lower cost:** No apostilles, certified translations, or international shipping of original documents
- **Faster setup:** 4-6 weeks vs potentially months for foreign entity registration
- **Better operations:** Mexican banks, suppliers, and authorities prefer working with local entities
- **Ring-fenced liability:** Mexican entity's obligations stay independent from foreign company
The foreign RFC route, while legally possible, involves significantly more complexity, cost, and risk.
*Source: Uriel's detailed comparison for Antonio/Amazon SPN*

### Q: Are there U.S. compliance requirements triggered by owning a Mexican entity?
**A:** Yes. U.S. shareholders may be subject to:
- **Form 5471 or Form 8865** (information return for foreign corporations)
- **GILTI tax** if applicable
- **FBAR and FATCA filings** if foreign bank accounts are held
- **Transfer pricing** documentation if intercompany transactions exist
We strongly recommend confirming obligations with a U.S. tax advisor familiar with international structures. Tally handles all Mexican-side compliance.
*Source: Real client question answered by Ximena/Uriel*

## CATEGORY 2: COFEPRIS & PRODUCT COMPLIANCE

### Q: What products require COFEPRIS approval in Mexico?
**A:** Any product that contacts the human body: dietary supplements, cosmetics, food products, beverages, medical devices, and pharmaceuticals. The regulation level and timeline varies significantly by category:
- **Cosmetics:** Low regulation, 3-15 days (Aviso de Funcionamiento)
- **Food & Beverages:** Medium, 1-3 months (Aviso + Import Permit)
- **Dietary Supplements:** High, 3-6 months (Aviso + Permiso Sanitario)
- **Supplements requiring Registro:** Very High, 6-12 months
- **Medical Devices (Class I-III):** Very High, 6-18 months
- **Pharmaceuticals:** Maximum, 12-24 months

### Q: What is a Viability Report and why do I need one?
**A:** A Viability Report is a preventive ingredient analysis Tally conducts BEFORE you invest in production or labeling. We analyze every ingredient against current COFEPRIS regulations and flag any prohibited or restricted substances. This catches problems in week one — not after your container is stuck at customs. For example, with our client GROWVE (12+ supplement brands), we caught two prohibited ingredients during the viability phase, saving them thousands in wasted production costs.

### Q: What happens with products containing melatonin?
**A:** In Mexico, all products containing melatonin are classified as **medications** (not supplements), requiring a Registro Sanitario from COFEPRIS before import. This applies regardless of how the product is classified in the US. The registration costs approximately $11,000 USD, is valid for 5 years, and multiple products with the same active ingredient, dose, and presentation can share a single registration to optimize costs. Monthly Responsable Sanitario cost of ~$450 USD also applies.
*Source: ProEmpire/Vitamatic client case — Alejandra/Samantha analysis*

### Q: What about probiotics — can they be imported?
**A:** Currently, COFEPRIS has determined that probiotics cannot be classified as dietary supplements and is NOT granting import permits for probiotic products entering Mexico. This is a regulatory restriction that may change, but for now we recommend not sending products with probiotics. We monitor regulatory changes and will advise when the situation evolves.
*Source: ProEmpire/Vitamatic client case*

### Q: How fast does Tally review labels?
**A:** Our in-house regulatory team reviews labels 5x faster than traditional firms — averaging ~25 labels per month for high-volume clients. We use PageProof for collaborative review and aim for 24-hour acknowledgment on urgent requests. For the GROWVE portfolio (12+ brands), we managed simultaneous label reviews across multiple product lines.

### Q: What is a Responsable Sanitario?
**A:** A licensed health professional legally required to supervise operations involving health products in Mexico. Must have a cedula profesional (professional license). Tally links qualified professionals to your entity on a monthly retainer (~$450 USD/month). Required for all companies handling COFEPRIS-regulated products.

### Q: Do I need NOM labeling in addition to COFEPRIS?
**A:** Yes. NOM labels are mandatory for ALL consumer products entering Mexico — they are separate from COFEPRIS permits. Key standards:
- **NOM-050:** General commercial information (required for all products)
- **NOM-141:** Cosmetics-specific labeling
- **NOM for electronics:** Safety standards
- **NOM for textiles:** Composition and labeling
Tally coordinates compliant Spanish-language oversticker labels for all applicable NOM standards.

## CATEGORY 3: IMPORTS & CUSTOMS

### Q: What are the import duties and taxes for Mexico?
**A:** Import costs include:
- **IGI (Import Duties):** 0-35% depending on tariff classification
- **IVA (Value-Added Tax):** 16% on declared value + duties (recoverable via tax filings)
- **DTA:** Customs processing fee (~0.8%)
- **Customs Broker Fees:** Variable by broker and volume
- **Warehouse & Handling:** Variable
- **Land Transport:** Variable by route
USMCA-origin products from the US or Canada may qualify for preferential rates of 0%. Tally provides a detailed cost breakdown before your first shipment — no surprises.

### Q: Does Tally import products directly?
**A:** No. Tally is a technology platform and strategic coordinator. We connect you with vetted, licensed customs brokers (agentes aduanales) from our partner network, assist with Padron de Importadores registration, and guide you through regulatory compliance. The actual import operations are performed by licensed third-party customs brokers and logistics providers. You contract directly with each provider.

### Q: What is the Padron de Importadores?
**A:** The Padron de Importadores is a mandatory registry with SAT that authorizes your company to import goods into Mexico. Without it, your entity cannot legally import. Certain product categories (textiles, footwear, chemicals) also require Padron Sectorial registration. Tally assists with both registration processes.

### Q: How does the T1 (test shipment) exemption work?
**A:** T1 allows a first test shipment through Tally's Texas consolidation center without needing your own IOR (Importer of Record) setup. It's designed for brands that want to test the Mexican market before committing to full import infrastructure. After T1, we recommend migrating to the IOR model under your own entity for ongoing operations, better duty rates, and VAT recovery.

### Q: Why should Chinese sellers avoid courier imports?
**A:** Courier imports (DHL, FedEx, etc.) to Mexico carry a 19% surcharge tariff. By importing through your own Mexican entity with a proper customs broker, you pay only the standard duty rate (often 0-15% depending on product). The savings are significant — this is one of the primary value propositions of the China Desk.

## CATEGORY 4: BANKING & FINANCE

### Q: How long does it take to open a Mexican bank account?
**A:** Bank account opening typically takes 2-4 weeks after entity formation is complete. The process includes KYC (Know Your Customer) documentation, PLD (anti-money laundering) compliance review, and sometimes in-person visits. Tally manages the full process with our partner banks: BBVA, Monex, Banco Base, Banorte, and Banamex. Both MXN and USD accounts are available.

### Q: Why do I need a Mexican bank account?
**A:** A Mexican bank account is essential for:
- Receiving marketplace payments (Amazon, Mercado Libre)
- Paying import duties, taxes, and suppliers
- Issuing and receiving CFDI invoices
- Payroll (if you have Mexican employees)
- Recovering VAT credits from imports
Without a bank account, your entity cannot operate commercially in Mexico.

### Q: What is VAT recovery and how does it work?
**A:** When you import goods into Mexico, you pay 16% IVA (VAT) at the border. This VAT is recoverable through your monthly tax filings — it becomes a credit against the VAT you collect on sales. This is a significant financial advantage of importing through your own entity vs. using a third-party importer. For example, on $100,000 worth of imports, you'd recover $16,000 in VAT credits.
*Source: Elizabeth's VAT explanation to GROWVE*

## CATEGORY 5: ACCOUNTING & TAX

### Q: What ongoing compliance requirements does my Mexican company have?
**A:** Included in Tally's service:
- Monthly and annual tax filings with SAT (even if company is inactive)
- Bookkeeping in compliance with Mexican regulations
- ISR (income tax), IVA (value-added tax), DIOT filings
- CFDI invoice issuance and management
- Foreign investment notifications (RNIE)
Additional services at extra cost: annual shareholder meetings, industry-specific filings, COFEPRIS-related permits.
*Source: Real client question answered by Ximena/Uriel*

### Q: What happens if my company has no revenue yet (e.g., waiting for COFEPRIS)?
**A:** Even with zero revenue, your Mexican entity has monthly filing obligations with SAT. Tally handles "contabilidad en ceros" (zero-balance accounting) — filing all required returns showing no activity. This keeps your company in good standing and avoids SAT penalties. Many clients operate in this state for 3-18 months while waiting for COFEPRIS permits.

### Q: How does Amazon invoicing work in Mexico?
**A:** Since January 2025, Mexico's SAT requires all foreign Amazon sellers to have a legal representative in Mexico. Amazon Mexico charges commissions on sales and issues CFDIs (electronic invoices) for their fees. These CFDIs must match your company's RFC and can be used to deduct expenses and recover VAT. If your RFC is not properly registered with Amazon, invoices may not be generated correctly, which affects your tax deductions.
