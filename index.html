Mexico Is the Opportunity.
The Bureaucracy Is the Barrier.

Mexico's ecommerce market hit $54.4 billion in 2025 and is projected to reach $175.8 billion by 2034. Amazon Mexico is growing at 34% year-over-year. 79% of Mexican consumers shop online. The opportunity is massive.

But launching here without local expertise? That's where brands get stuck.

Entity Formation & Tax Registration

Mexico requires a local entity with RFC (tax ID), eFirma (digital signature), and beneficial ownership declarations. One missed step delays everything by months.

COFEPRIS, NOM, SENASICA Compliance

Supplements, cosmetics, food, and medical devices need sanitary permits that take 6-18 months. Wrong ingredient? Your entire shipment gets rejected at customs.

Customs, Tariffs & Importer of Record

10-digit tariff codes. Padron de Importadores. Sectorial registrations. Red-light inspections. Each shipment is a compliance event.

Mexican Bank Account & Accounting

Foreign companies face enhanced KYC, PLD reviews, and monthly tax obligations (ISR, IVA, DIOT). Without a bank account, you can't receive payments or operate.

Without the right partner, a Mexico launch takes 12-18 months and costs 3x what it should. Most brands give up before they start selling.

Frequently Asked Questions

Since January 2025, Mexico's SAT requires all foreign Amazon sellers to have a legal representative in Mexico. In practice, this means you need: a Mexican legal entity (company), an RFC (tax ID), an eFirma (digital signature), a Mexican bank account, and depending on your product category, COFEPRIS permits or NOM certifications. Tally handles all of these as a single integrated service.

For products without COFEPRIS requirements (general merchandise, electronics, clothing), you can go from zero to selling in 8-12 weeks. This includes entity formation (4-6 weeks), banking (2-4 weeks parallel), first import (2-3 weeks), and marketplace setup.

Products requiring COFEPRIS sanitary registration (supplements, cosmetics, medical devices) require an additional 3-18 months for permit approval, though you can begin other steps immediately.

Any product that contacts the human body requires some form of COFEPRIS oversight: dietary supplements, cosmetics, food products, beverages, medical devices, and pharmaceuticals. The level of regulation varies — cosmetics can be cleared in 3-15 days, while medical devices may take 12-18 months. Our regulatory team conducts a Viability Report before you invest in production to catch any prohibited ingredients early.

Import costs include: customs duties (IGI, varying by product — 0% to 35%), 16% IVA (value-added tax on imports), DTA (customs processing fee, ~8‰), plus customs broker fees. Proper tariff classification can significantly reduce your effective duty rate. USMCA-origin products from the US or Canada may qualify for preferential duty rates. Our imports team provides a detailed cost quotation before your first shipment.
